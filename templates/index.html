de Waard–Milliams CO₂ Corrosion Model

The nominal corrosion rate V_nom is calculated via an Arrhenius-type formulation governed by temperature and CO₂ partial pressure. Correction factors are then applied to account for real pipeline conditions.

Key Correction Factors

  • F_sys — High-pressure gas non-ideality
  • F_scale — Protective FeCO₃ scale above T_scale
  • F_pH — Actual vs saturation pH of water phase
  • F_oil — Oil wetting inhibits water contact
  • F_cond — Water condensation rate in wet-gas
  • F_glyc — Glycol (MEG/DEG) inhibiting effect
  • F_inh — Chemical inhibitor efficiency

Ref: de Waard & Milliams, CORROSION/93, Paper 69.

▶ de Waard–Milliams Model Theory

Background

The de Waard–Milliams model (1975, revised 1993) is the industry-standard empirical method for predicting sweet (CO₂) corrosion in carbon steel pipelines. Developed from laboratory and field data across wide temperature and pressure ranges.

Core Mechanism

CO₂ dissolves in water forming carbonic acid (H₂CO₃), which drives iron dissolution. The nominal rate is:

V_nom = 10^(5.8 − 1710/T_K + 0.67 × log pCO₂)

Final Corrosion Rate

CR = V_nom × F_sys × F_scale × F_pH × F_oil × F_cond × F_glyc × F_inh

Validity & Limitations

  • Valid for pCO₂ up to ~10 bar, temperature 20–150 °C
  • Does not cover H₂S (sour corrosion) or erosion-corrosion
  • Does not cover microbiologically induced corrosion (MIC)
  • F_scale not valid if scale is mechanically damaged or removed
  • Always cross-check against field corrosion monitoring data

Reference: de Waard, C. & Milliams, D.E. (1975, updated 1993). Prediction of CO₂ corrosion of carbon steel. CORROSION/93, Paper 69.
